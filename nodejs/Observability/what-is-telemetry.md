# 📡 What Is Telemetry?

**Telemetry**  is the automated collection and transmission of data and measurements from distributed or remote sources to a central system for monitoring, analysis and resource optimization.

This data is produced **continuously**, while the system is live ⏱️  
Nothing is paused, nothing is inspected manually 🛑  
The system simply **reports what it is doing as it runs** 📢

---

## 📦 What Is Telemetry Data?

**Telemetry data** is the data emitted by the system in the form of the **three signals (pillars) of observability**.

In practice, telemetry data appears as:

* 📊 **Metrics telemetry**  
  Numbers that describe how the system behaves over time
  (load, speed, error counts, trends)

* 🪵 **Logs telemetry**  
  Recorded actions, decisions, and errors
  (what happened and why)

* 🧵 **Traces telemetry**  
  Request-level flow information
  (how a single request moved through the system)

These three forms together are what we call **telemetry data**.

---

## 🧠 Why This Matters

If a system emits **rich telemetry**:

* its behavior becomes visible 👀
* failures become explainable 🧩
* performance becomes measurable 📈

If it emits poor or no telemetry:

* it becomes a black box 📦
* debugging becomes guesswork 🎭

---

## 🎯 Summary

> **Telemetry is the continuous emission of metrics, logs, and traces from a running system, enabling observability.**
